SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT AND RELEASE

This Settlement Agreement and Release ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 15, 2024, 
by and between:

PLAINTIFF: Sarah Michelle Thompson, an individual residing at 4521 Maple Drive, 
Austin, Texas 78701, ("Plaintiff")

DEFENDANT: Meridian Healthcare Systems, Inc., a corporation organized and existing 
under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at 
1200 Corporate Boulevard, Suite 400, Dallas, Texas 75201, ("Defendant")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Plaintiff filed a civil action in the United States District Court for the 
Western District of Texas, Case No. 24-CV-00891, styled Thompson v. Meridian Healthcare 
Systems, Inc., alleging claims of wrongful termination, disability discrimination under 
the Americans with Disabilities Act (42 U.S.C. ยง 12101 et seq.), and retaliation under 
the Family and Medical Leave Act (29 U.S.C. ยง 2601 et seq.);

WHEREAS, Defendant denies all allegations of wrongdoing;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to resolve all disputes between them without further 
litigation;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth 
herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. SETTLEMENT PAYMENT
Defendant agrees to pay Plaintiff the total sum of THREE HUNDRED FIFTY THOUSAND 
DOLLARS ($350,000.00), payable as follows:
   a. $250,000.00 within thirty (30) days of execution of this Agreement
   b. $100,000.00 within sixty (60) days of execution of this Agreement

2. CONFIDENTIALITY
Plaintiff agrees to keep the terms of this Agreement strictly confidential and shall 
not disclose the settlement amount or terms to any person or entity, except to 
Plaintiff's attorneys, accountants, or as required by law.

3. NON-DISPARAGEMENT
Both parties agree to refrain from making any disparaging, negative, or defamatory 
statements about the other party to any third party.

4. RELEASE OF CLAIMS
Plaintiff hereby releases and forever discharges Defendant from any and all claims, 
demands, causes of action, and liabilities of any kind whatsoever, known or unknown, 
arising out of or related to Plaintiff's employment with Defendant.

5. NO ADMISSION OF LIABILITY
Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as an admission of liability or wrongdoing 
by either party.

6. GOVERNING LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the 
State of Texas, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

7. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to 
the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, 
warranties, and understandings.
