Attorney Quick-Start Guide
1  —  Getting Started
Step 1 Open Amicus

Double-click the Amicus shortcut on your desktop, or run launch.sh from the Amicus folder. Your browser will open automatically.

Step 2 Create your case

Click New Case in the left sidebar. Enter the case name (e.g. Smith v. Jones). Click Create.

Step 3 Upload documents

Click Upload Documents in the sidebar. Select your PDF, Word, or text files. Amicus will process them — this takes 1–2 minutes per document.

2  —  Asking Questions

Type your question exactly as you would ask a colleague:

  • What did the defendant say about the contract signing date?
  • Are there any contradictions between the witness statements?
  • What damages are being claimed and how were they calculated?
  • Summarize all accommodation requests made by the plaintiff.
3  —  Reading Your Results
› Amicus shows its answer with the exact source documents cited. Always verify important facts against the original documents before relying on them in court.
› The confidence badge — High Medium Low — reflects how well the answer matches the source documents. High means the cited sources directly support the answer.
› The Sources section lists every document consulted to build the answer, with a relevance score.
4  —  Analysis Modes
● Basic Fast answers. Best for simple lookups.
● Advanced More thorough. Recommended for most questions.
● Expert Deepest analysis. Best for complex multi-document questions. Takes approximately 30 seconds longer.
🔒

Your files stay on your computer. Amicus runs entirely on your computer. Your case files are never uploaded to the internet or shared with any third party. All analysis happens locally.
